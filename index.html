About

I am interested in defining and solving problems in the training and evaluation of AI Agents toward real-world deployment. Previously, I've conducted research on dialogue interaction between AI Agents and human users, and I still have great enthusiasm for it. Beyond this, I am interested in RL training of AI Agents and visually-grounded Agents (e.g., GUI, Mobile agents).

Publications

[1] ICLR 2026
Non-Collaborative User Simulators for Tool Agents
Jeonghoon Shim, Woojung Song, Cheyon Jin, Seungwon Koo, and Yohan Jo
  • Identification of non-collaborative user behaviors observed in real-world customer service
  • Proposal of simulation methods that replicate non-collaborative users
  • Experimental results demonstrate that state-of-the-art and fine-tuned agents can be impaired when faced with non-collaborative users
[2] ICLR 2025
ToolDial: Multi-turn Dialogue Generation Method for Tool-Augmented Language Models
Jeonghoon Shim, Gyuhyeon Seo, Cheongsu Lim, and Yohan Jo
  • Proposal of a syntactic dialogue data generation method for tool agents utilizing an API graph and agenda-based approach
  • Automatic generation of multi-turn dialogues covering diverse scenarios in terms of quality and quantity
  • Experimental results reveal that state-of-the-art tool agents struggle in perceiving exact input parameter states and optimal action selection throughout multi-turn dialogue
[3] KST 2022
Analysis of Seoul Public Bike Demand during Night Times by Applying Heckman Selection Models
Jeonghoon Shim and Joonho Ko
  • Analysis of shared bicycle usage pattern differences between late-night and daytime hours in Seoul
  • Application of Heckman Selection models on public bike usage data with geospatial features
  • Discovery that the current placement of shared bicycle stations may be misaligned with the needs of late-night users
